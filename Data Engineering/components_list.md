#Components Data

1. LM393 Comparator IC
The LM393 is a dual differential comparator used to compare two input voltages. In this project, it compares the input voltage with a preset reference voltage and produces an output indicating whether the threshold level is crossed.
Specifications: Supply voltage 2V–36V, low power consumption, open collector output.

2. Resistor (R1)
Value: 10 kΩ
Function: Works as part of a voltage divider network to generate the reference voltage for the comparator.

3. Resistor (R2)
Value: 10 kΩ
Function: Forms the second part of the voltage divider used to set the threshold voltage.

4. Resistor (R3)
Value: 10 kΩ
Function: Acts as a pull-up resistor for the comparator output to ensure proper logic level output.

5. Resistor (R4 / R5)
Value: 220 Ω – 1 kΩ
Function: Limits the current flowing through the LEDs to protect them from damage.

6. Potentiometer (RV1)
Value: 10 kΩ
Function: Used to adjust the reference voltage level. By rotating the potentiometer, the voltage threshold can be varied.

7. Light Emitting Diodes (LEDs)
Type: Red and Green indicator LEDs
Function: Provide visual indication of the voltage level. When the input voltage crosses the threshold, the LED turns ON.

8. Power Supply
Voltage: 5V – 9V DC
Function: Provides the required power to operate the comparator circuit and indicator LEDs.

9. Connector (J1)
Type: 2-Pin Connector
Function: Used to supply input voltage and ground connection to the circuit.
